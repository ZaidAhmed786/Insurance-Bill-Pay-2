Liability Coverage

Protect yourself and others. Liability coverage helps cover third-party injuries and property damage in case of an accident where you're at fault. Essential in almost every state.

Collision Coverage

Covers repair or replacement costs for your vehicle after accidents. Ideal for leased or financed vehicles, giving you financial protection no matter who’s at fault.

Comprehensive Coverage

Safeguard against theft, vandalism, fire, natural disasters, or animal damage. Comprehensive coverage ensures your car is protected beyond collisions.

Medical Payments Coverage

Covers medical expenses for you and your passengers, regardless of fault. A strong option to ease financial stress after unexpected accidents.

Personal Injury Protection (PIP)

Go beyond medical costs. PIP can help cover lost wages, childcare, and other accident-related expenses, available in select states.

Driver Coverage Protection

Protect yourself from uninsured or underinsured drivers. This coverage ensures financial safety when others on the road aren’t adequately insured.

Frequently Asked Questions

Explore our FAQs for concise answers to common auto insurance queries, simplifying your decision-making process.

What Does Car Insurance Cover? ▼
  • Depending on the coverages selected, car insurance can typically cover damage or loss to your vehicle, liability for bodily injury or property damage you may cause to others, and medical payments for injuries sustained in a covered accident.
  • Additionally, you may be able to add optional coverages for things like roadside assistance, rental car reimbursement, vehicle towing, and more.
  • However, coverage varies depending on your specific policy and insurance company.
  • Review your policy and speak with your insurance agent to fully understand your coverage.
How Much Does Car Insurance Cost? ▼
  • The cost of auto insurance can vary widely depending on a variety of factors such as the insurance company's rates, your age, driving record, vehicle type, location, and policy type.
  • Additionally, the level of coverage and deductibles you choose can also affect the cost of your auto insurance.
  • Compare quotes from multiple insurance companies to get an idea of the cost of coverage for your specific needs.
What is Gap Insurance For Cars? ▼
  • Gap insurance coverage is designed to help protect drivers who have financed or leased their vehicle.
  • In the event that a financed or leased vehicle is totaled or stolen, many car insurance policies will typically only cover the market value of the vehicle at the time of the incident. This amount may be less than what the driver owes on the vehicle.
  • Gap insurance can help cover the difference between the market value of a vehicle and the remaining amount owed on the loan or lease.
Is Car Insurance Required By Law? ▼
  • Most states require some level of auto insurance coverage to be maintained, with many states requiring a minimum amount of liability coverage.
  • Auto insurance coverage that may be required by a state includes liability, uninsured/underinsured motorist, personal injury protection (PIP), and medical payments coverage. Be sure to check your state's requirements to ensure compliance with applicable laws.
What is a Deductible In Car Insurance? ▼
  • A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket on a covered claim before the insurance applies to cover its portion.
  • For example, if the covered claim amount is $2,000 and your deductible is $500, you'd pay $500 of the claim, and the insurance company would pay the remaining $1,500.
  • A higher deductible may result in a lower premium.
